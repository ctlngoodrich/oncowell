# Need Statement

## Problem

Patients facing complex treatment decisions (e.g., surgery vs. radiation) struggle to understand medical terminology, evaluate their options in the context of their own situation, assess provider quality, and identify the right questions to ask — leading to feeling overwhelmed and under-informed at the point of decision.

## Population

Older adults (60s+) who are independent, want to be in control of their healthcare decisions, are comfortable with basic technology, and rely on a family caregiver to help organize and interpret medical information.

## Outcome

The patient can clearly articulate their treatment options, understand the tradeoffs in plain language, evaluate where and from whom to receive care, and walk into a doctor's appointment with informed, specific questions — feeling confident rather than overwhelmed.
